3 Introduction

Welcome to class! This website is designed to accompany Mason Garrison’s Data Science for Psychologists graduate course at Wake Forest University. It is both the syllabus and primary textbook.

3.1 Course Description

This applied course is designed for psychologists with limited programming experience. It introduces the basic tennents of data science and links them to traditional psychological approaches. and provides a means to e

Along the way, we will develop an understanding of R programming as well as open science practices. In general, data science focuses on exploratory analyses and description rather than on corfirmatory analyses and prediction. Through the semester we will work our way thru Wickham and Grolemund’s R for Data Science text and develop proficiency with tidyverse.

This is an applied course for social scientists with little-to-no programming experience who wish to harness growing digital and computational resources. The focus of the course is on generating reproducible research through the use of programming languages and version control software. Major emphasis is placed on a pragmatic understanding of core principles of programming and packaged implementations of methods. Students will leave the course with basic computational skills implemented through many computational methods and approaches to social science; while students will not become expert programmers, they will gain the knowledge of how to adapt and expand these skills as they are presented with new questions, methods, and data.

3.2 Broad Topics

This class cover the following broad five areas: * Reproducibility; * Replication; * Robust Methods; * Resplendent Visualizations; and * R Programming.
